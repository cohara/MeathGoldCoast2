Boyne Viaduct


The Viaduct crossing the river Boyne was built in 1855. The first trains came to Drogheda in 1844. Passengers to Belfast were taken by horsedrawn vehicles across the Boyne to a station at Newfoundwell on the north side to be entrained to continue their journey north. At the time of construction it was the largest span bridge of its kind in the world. It consists of 12 masonry arches on the Dublin side, 3 wrought iron girder spans over the river Boyne and3 masonry arches on the north side.
